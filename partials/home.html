GMO Food

Genetically Modified (GM) Foods have foreign genes added to their original genetic codes and thus are "Genetically Modified or Genetically Engineered".

As with anything that exists, there are risks and disadvantages to GMO food consumption but it is not going away anytime soon.
"Genetically modified foods have been linked to toxic and allergic reactions, sickness, sterile and dead livestock, and damage to virtually every organ studied in lab animals," according to the Institute for Responsible Technology, a group of anti-GMO activists.

Also, a lot of developed countries believe GMO Foods are not safe for consumption but we cannot deny that it has advantages: "GM crops can improve yields for farmers, reduce draws on natural resources and fossil fuels and provide nutritional benefits," according to a statement on the website for Monsanto, the world's largest manufacturer of GMOs.
